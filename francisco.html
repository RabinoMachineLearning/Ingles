Francisco Brennand Sculpture Park

The Francisco Brennand Sculpture Park is an open-air art museum located in the city of Recife, capital of the Brazilian state of Pernambuco. It is one of the main achievements of Pernambuco artist Francisco Brennand. Created in the 1990s as part of the project “I saw the world… It began in Recife” to celebrate the 500th anniversary of the discovery of Brazil, the park was inaugurated on December 29, 2000. In total, there are 90 sculptures created by Francisco Brennand , the main work in the complex being the Crystal Tower, 32 meters high and made of clay and bronze. Despite being one of the main tourist attractions in Recife, in a survey carried out by Jornal do Comércio in 2018, it was understood that more than half of the population does not know about the park. In 2014, all works were restored, as well as the park, in addition to receiving reinforcements in the complex's security.
