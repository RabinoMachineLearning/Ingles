Ciranda

Ciranda is one of the most traditional dances in Brazil, being very popular in the state of Pernambuco. It is especially loved on the Island of Itamarac√°, where it appeared according to some historians who claim that it was inspired by the movements of sea waves.

The beginning of the ciranda in Brazil The first studies on ciranda in Brazil were carried out around the 1960s. They point out that the initial milestone in the country would have occurred around the 18th century, especially in the Northeast region. According to Jaime Diniz, priest, musicologist and pioneer in research on ciranda in Pernambuco, the predominance occurred in Mata Norte and on the coast of that state. The composition was an interaction between singing and dancing. The themes of the lyrics were not fixed. In general, they talked about agriculture, the countryside, nature, love, beaches, etc. Initially, cirandas were practiced by rural workers, fishermen and construction workers, on the seashore, in the streets and in yards. This caused them to be associated with the popular classes and characterized as a genuine expression of the people.
